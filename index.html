Doing Exerything?

What message are you sending to your children? .

Besides the practical benefits of everyone mucking in, children have psychological-developmental needs related to doing chores. It’s healthy for kids to contribute at home. Being a part of the ongoing work of running a household helps children develop an awareness of the needs of others, while at the same time contributing to their own emotional well being

Belonging and Pride

Children who consider themselves necessary to the family are less likely to feel adrift in a world where a core human need is to feel needed. Children who earn their privelleges rather than just being automatically entitled to them are more likely to value what they have. The best predictor for young adults success in mid 20’s is whether they participated in household tasks age 3/4.

Start building lifelong Habits now

Getting children to do chores is often far from easy but it’s actually quite simple: insist and persist. When new chores are assigned it’s beneficial to show your kids how them how to do them, and do the chores with them the first few times until they get the hang of it. Then give them the responsibility to do the chores on their own. Strive to not buy into excuses and accept that you may need to repeat yourself over and over.

Children should do chores . . . . Period.

The arguement that a child is too young has been disproven, multiple studies point to the fact that mucking in with developmentally appropriate tasks will give your child the best start in life. It equips them with essential life skills that grow gradually as they do, gives them a greater sense of responsibility and accomplishment and teaches them to exist and interact appropriately in society. What is not fair to a child is not arming them with these essential skills, then expecting them to launch in the adult world fully formed. Doing everything for and on behalf of a child teaches them a level of entitlement that cannot be sustained outside the family unit and will cause then problems as they grow up and interact with the wider community.
What are you waiting for? NOW is the best time to start
